# R&B

## Overview
R&B (rhythm and blues) is a genre rooted in African American musical traditions, blending soul, funk, gospel, and hip-hop influences. Modern R&B emphasizes smooth vocals, expressive melodies, and intimate emotional storytelling, often centered on themes of love, heartbreak, and self-reflection.

## Mood and Energy
R&B typically operates in low-to-medium energy ranges with soulful, introspective moods. Tempos commonly fall between 60 and 100 BPM, supporting a relaxed, emotional listening experience.

## Listening Contexts
Listeners turn to R&B for late-night reflection, romantic settings, quiet drives, and solo emotional processing. The genre suits intimate environments more than high-energy social spaces.

## Notable Characteristics
Distinguishing features include layered vocal harmonies, prominent bass lines, subtle electronic production, and lyrics that prioritize emotional vulnerability over narrative storytelling.
